You stare at the screen wondering what class to take and whether you value your sanity more than your desire to learn something you want to. I'm sure everyone's been there at some point in their lives whether it be in high school or college. Colleges have a way of disincentivizing taking challenging classes and students have figured this out ages ago. When asked why one would take a course whose name you struggle to even remember their answer will probably be "It's an easy A".

<!-- more -->

Before we dive into the imbalance itself, let's take a moment and talk about the grading system in colleges across the United States. Classes usually have a number of credits associated with them, indicating the number of class hours. At the end of each period of study whether it be a quarter or a semester the students are assigned a grade. This grade is supposed to be an evaluation of how well the student performed in the course and take into account various aspects of the academics - participation, homework, exams, projects, etc. The grade will usually be a letter grade despite the fact that all the grades one has received throughout the semester are a percentage. This means that the grade is a rough estimation which is perfectly fine as it relieves some pressure of academics. The letter grade in turn will contribute to a student's grade point average (GPA).

Now that we've established some basis, let's start first with how students are graded.
